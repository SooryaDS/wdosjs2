FUN THINGS TO KNOW ABOUT TURTLES!


  1. Around 250 different species of turtles can be found worldwide:



    2. Turtles come in a huge variety of species. Sea turtle species in Sri lanka include, among others:
    • Olive Ridley (Lepidochelys olivacea)
    • Green (Chelonia mydas)
    • Hawksbill (Eretmochelys imbricata)
    • Loggerhead (Caretta caretta)
    • Leatherback (Dermochelys coriacea) turtles.

    There are numerous turtle species that are found all over the world. Turtles can be found in bays, lagoons, estuaries, oceans, open seas, and shallow coastal waters Turtles can be found on land, in freshwater, and in the sea.




  2. Sea turtles can't pull their heads back into their shell like other turtles can when faced with danger.



    3. The heads and flippers of sea turtles, however, cannot be retracted inside their shells. The "sidenecked turtles" and the "hidden neck turtles" are the two main categories for turtle species that have the ability to retract their heads.


    The manner the head retracts serves as the basis for this classification. The sidenecked turtle folds its neck to the side while retracting, whereas the hidden neck turtle retracts its head straight back into its shell.




  3. Turtles are unable to leave their shell.


    4. The reptile order Testudines includes turtles. All turtle species have bony shells covering their entire bodies. The plastron is the lower shell, and the carapace is the top shell. Their rib and spinal column mature into the bony shells. A skeletal box is created when the upper and lower shells are joined.


    A turtle can never emerge from its shell. The bones and cartilage that make up the turtle's shell are an essential component of its body.
